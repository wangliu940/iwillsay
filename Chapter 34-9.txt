On the night of April 9, 2015, police in Paris were called to the scene of a fight that began after a man tried to punch a woman who had been in a car that was parked outside a hospital. The woman was struck multiple times, and the assailant, a man in his mid-20s, was shot and killed by police. Following the initial investigation, the national police chief, Emmanuel Amoureux, publicly blamed the man who had been stabbed on the police and said the man should be arrested.

The next day, July 4, 2015, police in Brussels were called to the scene of a fight in which a man was punched once in the face by an officer while he was in a hospital.
