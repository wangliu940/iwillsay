Live updates: Latest on the deadly Los Angeles wildfires - CNN's Jeff Zeleny and Nick Merrill report

LAS VEGAS -- The Los Angeles fire raged through the night, with the most severe blaze affecting about 130 homes and businesses, according to the Los Angeles Fire Department.

The department identified the blaze as a Category 4 fire at a site near the intersection of South Highway and E. 7th Street in the 800 block of E. 7th Street.

The firefighters said there was a fire burning at the site of the blaze, but no injuries.

The fire was reported at about 6 p.m. by a contractor working at the site.

The contractor did not return a request for comment.

The fire is expected to grow to 11 to 12 acres by the end of the day, the blaze's chief firefighter, Sgt. Steve Felt, told the Los Angeles Times.

Felt said he had no information about how the fire was burning or if it had started in a house or apartment.
