European region sees highest number of measles cases in 25 years, WHO says - CNN

More than 1,600 people died from measles in the first 24 hours of the outbreak in the southern Indian state of Assam, according to the World Health Organisation.

More than 1,600 people died from measles last month in the state, the World Health Organisation said, in the first 24 hours of the outbreak, which began late Monday.

The WHO said there were more than 1,600 cases in the state and more than 6,000 cases in the neighbouring state of Assam, which is home to more than 300,000 people.

The WHO said the outbreak is the worst since 1996 in the region.

The virus, which is spread through contact with skin by infected animals, is transmitted by contact with people. It is also transmitted by coughing or sneezing.
